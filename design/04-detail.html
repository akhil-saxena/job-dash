D1: Hero + Tabs

Sticky hero section at top with company name, role, status dropdown, pin/archive actions, and a full meta row (priority, source, date, salary, location, days, tags). Below the hero: an underline tab bar switches between Overview, Interviews, JD, Docs, and Timeline panels.

  • Overview shows two-column grid: editable fields on left, notes on right
  • Hero gives immediate context without scrolling
  • Familiar tab pattern reduces cognitive load
  • Mobile: hero compresses, tabs scroll horizontally
  • Trade-off: hero takes vertical space; deep content requires tab switching

D2: Sidebar Info

Company info lives in a persistent left sidebar panel -- status, priority, source, salary, dates, tags, all visible and editable at all times. Main area on right holds tabs (Notes, Interviews, JD, Docs, Timeline). No hero needed.

  • Info is always visible regardless of which tab is active
  • Sidebar collapses to a summary strip on mobile (company + status + priority)
  • Feels like a CRM or issue tracker (Jira, Linear)
  • Good for frequent status/field updates while reading notes
  • Trade-off: sidebar eats horizontal space on smaller desktops

D3: Single Scroll

No tabs at all. Everything on one scrollable page in sequential sections: header with status and meta, Notes, Interview Rounds, JD Snapshot, Documents, Timeline. Sections separated by uppercase labels.

  • Simplest mental model -- no tab switching, just scroll
  • Everything visible with a single scroll gesture
  • Mobile: identical layout, just narrower. No adaptation needed
  • Good when applications don't have tons of content per section
  • Trade-off: long page if many interview rounds or large JD
