D3 — Feed + Right Panel

D1 — Stats-First

Classic dashboard. 4 big stat cards at top, two-column lists below (stale apps + interviews), activity feed at bottom. You see numbers first.

Best for: Quick overview, metrics-oriented users
Risk: Stats aren't actionable — "24 active" doesn't tell you what to do
proven patternmetrics-firstfamiliar

D2 — Action-First

Hero greeting with urgency alerts (interview today, stale apps, expiring offer). Stats are smaller and secondary. Three-column grid below. You see what needs doing first.

Best for: "What needs my attention right now?" — matches your core value
Risk: Hero card takes vertical space. Greeting can feel gimmicky over time
urgency-drivenaction-orientedTodoist-like

D3 — Feed + Panel

Activity feed as the main content (chronological, rich detail). Right panel shows today's schedule, attention items, and deadlines. Like an inbox with a sidebar.

Best for: Staying updated on what changed, catching up on pipeline activity
Risk: Three-column (sidebar + feed + panel) may feel cramped. Activity is passive, not action-oriented
inbox feelcontext-richSlack-like
